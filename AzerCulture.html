Azerbaijan


Azerbaijan is a Turkic-speaking country located between Europe and Asia. It is widely known for its import of oil and natural gas.

What is Culture of Azerbaijan about

The ornamental and applied arts are essential components of Azerbaijani culture. Numerous handicrafts, including chasing, jewelry, metal engraving, wood, stone, and bone carving, carpet making, pattern weaving and printing, knitting, and embroidery, serve as representations of them. Music and dancing are also an essential part of people's culture, there are a lot of instruments, compositions, and dancing styles created by the nation. Different celebrations also represent this country's traditions and culture.

Main historical periods of Azerbaijan

There were a lot of significant periods during history that primarily affected Azerbaijan's cultures and traditions. The switch in religion and actions of different governments has led to changes in the culture of the nation. Caucasian Albania is believed to be the first state in modern-day Azerbaijan’s territories. After Muslim Arabs defeated the Sassanid and Byzantine empires they entered the Caucasus. Most of Azerbaijan was converted to Islam and it still remains the major religion in the country. Later many dynasties were established in Azerbaijan's history, the Safavid empire was one of them, it was first founded in 1501 and Shia Islam started to rise, in the 21st century Shia Islam still claims to be the largest religion in the country. After two wars between Russia and Qajars the Treaty of Gulistan and the Treaty of Turkmenchay were signed. In terms of the treaties Qajars had to leave Caucasian territories, and Azerbaijani khanates were involved in Russia. Until April 28 1918 Azerbaijan was under Russian rule. In 1918 the Azerbaijan Democratic Republic was created for the first time. Shortly After 2 years on April 28, 1920, Azerbaijan ceased to be independent of the Soviet Union. 71 years Azerbaijan was under Soviet rule and only in October 1991 October 18 it could gain its independence back.

    Music/Instruments/Compositions

    There are various types of Azerbaijani music and each is unique.They include Classical music, Opera and Ballet, Folk Music, Ashiqs, Meykhana, Mugham, Azerbaijani rap,hiphop,jazz and rock. Modern times music is not really consideret as a part of the culture yet even with their great popularity.Baku Academy of Music was established to provide classical artists with the same support as folk musicians in 1920, when Azerbaijani classical music had experienced an upsurge.When Azerbaijani musicians first encountered European musical traditions throughout the Imperial Russian and Soviet eras of the country's history, opera and ballet began to flourish in Azerbaijan. Opera singers from Azerbaijan who achieved international recognition include Bulbul, Shovkat Mammadova, Fatma Mukhtarova, Huseyngulu Sarabski, Hagigat Rzayeva, Rashid Behbudov, Rauf Atakishiyev, Muslim Magomayev, Fidan and Khuraman Gasimovas, Rubaba Muradova, and Zeynab Khanlarova.The majority of songs are historical accounts of actual situations or tales from Azerbaijani folklore, or they are the result of competitions amongst medieval poets. Folk songs are typically played during weddings, funerals, and unique celebrations, in keeping with their origins.Ashiqs are traveling bards who sing and play the saz. Their tunes are semi-improvised Around a shared basis. It is  regarded as a sign of national identity and considered a protector of the Azerbaijani language, literature, and music. Meykhana is a particular literary and folk rap genre, consists of an improvised song played by one or more individuals without any musical accompaniment about a specific topic. Because meykhana is done in rhythm to a beat, it differs from spoken word poetry.. Mugham modes also refer to a collection of melodies and melodic fragments that are passed down orally and used by artists during improvisation. Mugham is a complex, multi-part composition. The selection of a certain mugham and performing style is suitable for a certain situation. Increasing intensity and rising pitches are frequently linked to the dramatic development of a performance, as well as a type of poetic-musical communication between artists and engaged audiences.

    Instruments

    Azerbaijani musical insturments include

  • Tar
    • Azerbaijani Tar was made at the end of the 18th century. It was a better version of Persian Tar which was created before. Azerbaijani Tar has 11 strings while Persian tar has only 6, in addition when playing Azerbaijani tar the singer puts it near their chest and mostly slightly bend the head towards it.
  • Kamancha
    • his writings, Abdulqadir Maraghi also made mention to kamancha and gave details about it. The kamancha is a three-stringed instrument with a particularly lovely sound timbre, according to E. Kempfer, a German explorer who visited Azerbaijan in the 18th century. Kamancha is made up of a body, an arm, and a bar that is crossed inside the body and joins the two pieces together. The walnut wood used for the body, arm, and tuning pegs was specially honed using machine tools. Sturgeon skin covers the body's outside. The proper measurement of the distance between the instrument's strings and arm is crucial to a high-quality sound output.
  • Balaban
    • Balaban was used since 1931 in Azerbaijani orchestra, at the time when the Folk Instrument Orchestra was established in Azerbijan. A percussionist plays in combination with a Balaban player at ceremonial events like weddings and holiday rituals. "Balabanchilar Dastasi" is the name of a traditional Azerbaijani musical ensemble made up of two balaban musicians and a percussionist.
  • Berbet
    • Performed with a mizrab, this stringed instrument was used in Azerbaijan in the 16th and 17th century. Berbet, an instrument in the family of oud-like instruments, has a larger body than an oud and a longer arm. Berbet in particular is regarded as the palace instrument, and Nizami Ganjavi's creativity and works of Azerbaijani classic literature both give knowledge of it.
  • Saz
    • Saz is one of the emblems of Turkic morals and the Turkic culture. Being syncretic,singing, poetry, speech and theatrical performances, acting and dance arts, it is also linked to mugham art and, at the same time, old elements of Azerbaijan's national culture. In terms of musical technique and sound acoustics, Azerbaijani saz varies from saz and other instruments common among Turkic-speaking peoples. These differences have definite benefits.Saz's arm is composed of walnut wood, while its body is constructed from certain varieties of mulberry trees. Wooden ribs linked to each other in an odd number form the body of the sculpture. An arm is added after the ribs are put together on tiny "kup" pieces that link the arm and torso. A thin wooden cover is placed over the body's surface, and 16-17 curtains are connected to the arm.
  • Ghaval
    • The gaval is a traditional percussion instrument from medieval Azerbaijan.The instrument's earliest music scores were created by Uzeyir Hajibayli, a brilliant Azerbaijani composer.Gaval, which has maintained its original shape over the ages, has blended into Azerbaijani culture and holds a distinctive position in the nation's musical legacy.Gaval is a membranophone instrument, but it also contains certain characteristics of an idiophone instrument, such as metal rings and tiny jingles that generate a distinctive sound when shaken.Gaval is also a crucial component of the "Mugham trio," which also consists of the instruments tar and kamancha and is necessary for the playing of the mugham.
  • Nagara
    • it may be played with other instruments and in groups, nagara is most frequently played with the zourna, a wind instrument. The nagara comes in a variety of sizes and is known by a variety of names, including "boyuk nagara" (large nagara), "cura nagara" (little nagara), "chiling nagara" (performed with drumsticks), "Qoltuq nagara" (drum placed under the arm), "el naghara,"(hand nagara) and "gosha nagara" (Double Nagara or Naqareh)
  • Zourna
    • Specifically, zourna is frequently employed in open-air folk festivities. This instrument has historically existing forms of black zourna, Arabi zourna, Jura zourna, Ajami zourna, qaba zourna, and shahabi zourna. It is played specifically in wind instrument ensembles. It is utilized in various dances and other musical compositions as a solo instrument, as part of an ensemble of folk instruments, and in orchestral compositions. Uzeir Hajibayov has incorporated it into the orchestral composition of the Koroglu opera.

    Novruz holiday can be concidered as one of the greatest celebrations in Azerbaijan

    Novruz is a holiday in Azerbaijan which celebrates the coming of spring, it has been celebrated in Azerbaijan throughout the centuries.Novruz preparations often get underway a month before the celebration. People celebrate the day of one of the four elements, water, fire, wind, or soil each Tuesday. In addition to preparing a wide range of ethnic foods, people also clean their homes, plant plants, sew new clothes, paint eggs, and prepare national sweets like shekerbura, pakhlava, and shorgoghal. Every Tuesday during the four weeks leading up to the celebration, youngsters jump over little bonfires, and candles are lighted as a respect to pre-Islamic Zoroastrian traditions. Kosa and Kechel are the characters of Novruz who are found in big centres of celebrations, they tell kids fairytales, play with them, dance and do other traditional Novruz activities.The graves of relatives are visited and cared for on the night before the holiday.At musical gatherings, different traditional games and performances like "Kos-Kosa," egg fights, arm wrestling, and fortune telling take place. Folk musicians perform tunes while wrestlers put their strength to the test. Novruz is a family holiday and it is very important to spend your time with your family on this beautiful event . The evening before the celebration, the entire family gathers around the festive table, which has been set with a variety of foods to make the New Year prosperous. The celebration lasts for many days and is concluded with public dancing, national sports competitions, and further entertainment from folk bands, among many other things. Khoncha is the name for the celebratory table's adornment. Candles and colored eggs are arranged on a large silver or copper tray in the middle of which are Samani green, shoots from wheat seeds.The first Charshanba is thought to symbolise water and celebrate its cleansing properties. Fire is the subject of the second Charshanba. People are said to be renewed and cleaned of disease by jumping over bonfires and lighting candles, enabling them to begin the spring with optimism. According to Azerbaijani legend, the third Charshanba is wind, which heralds the arrival of spring. People in some parts of western Azerbaijan refer to this day as "Black Wednesday." People from Azerbaijan are required to visit and maintain the graves of their relatives on this day."Torpag Chershenbesi" the fourth and last Charshanba, stands for the earth. It is thought that on this day, nature comes back to life.The people of Azerbaijan have unique customs associated to this Charshanba and see it as the most significant of the four holidays. For instance, the table should have seven different types of food, all of which names must begin with the letter "s" in Azerbaijani. Children knock on the doors of their neighbors' homes and leave their hats or bags in the hopes of receiving candy and holiday-specific treats like pakhlava and shakarbura Carpetmaking

    The weaving of Azerbaijani carpets is a historical and cultural practice of the Azerbaijani people. The Azerbaijani carpet is a regionally distinctive traditional handmade textile with designs unique to each of Azerbaijan's several carpet-making areas. It comes in a variety of sizes, has a thick texture, and either a pile-covered or pile-free surface. Making carpets is a family custom that is passed down verbally and through practice.Every young girl was required to master the craft of carpet weaving in the past, and the carpets she produced were a component of her dowry. A son who had just been married had his mother weave a big rug for his new home
